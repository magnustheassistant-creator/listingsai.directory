Affiliate Disclosure

In plain English: ListingsAI contains affiliate links. When you click one and sign up for a tool, we may earn a commission. It costs you nothing extra. The company pays us out of their marketing budget.

"Some links on this site are affiliate links. If you sign up for a service through one of these links, ListingsAI may receive a commission at no additional cost to you."

— Standard FTC-compliant disclosure, used across every page of ListingsAI where affiliate links appear. (16 CFR Part 255)

How this works

When you click an affiliate link on ListingsAI and sign up for the tool we recommended, the company tracks that you came from us. If you become a paying customer, they pay us a referral fee — usually a percentage of what you pay them, sometimes a flat fee, sometimes both.

The price you pay is the same whether you use our link or go directly to the tool's website. The commission comes out of the company's marketing budget, not your pocket.

This model — sometimes called affiliate marketing — is how independent review sites like ours stay funded. We don't run ads, we don't sell your data, we don't take sponsored posts. The affiliate commissions are our primary revenue source.

What this means for you

  • The price doesn't change. You pay the same whether you use our link or not.
  • Some tools offer sign-up bonuses through our link (extended trials, free months, etc.). These are negotiated by us on your behalf — you wouldn't get them going direct.
  • You're supporting independent review work. Our research, testing, and writing is paid for by these commissions.
  • No spam, no data sharing. We don't share your email with the tools we link to unless you explicitly opt in.

Our editorial standards

Earning a commission does not change which tools we recommend. We have a strict policy:

  • We don't accept payment for placements. A tool can't pay to be listed or to get a higher rating.
  • We don't write sponsored reviews. Every review is our own opinion, based on our testing.
  • We call out the bad stuff. If a tool has problems, we say so, even if it means recommending a competitor.
  • We disclose affiliate links on every page. Look for the "Affiliate Disclosure" footer on any page with recommendations.

Any tool we recommend is recommended because it can help you earn or save money — not because it will. We don't make income guarantees. We don't say "you'll make $5,000 guaranteed." Realistically:
