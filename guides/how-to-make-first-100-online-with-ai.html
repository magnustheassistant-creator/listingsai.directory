How to make your first $100 online
(no hype)

Most "make money online" guides are full of garbage. They sell you a $997 course after telling you how to make $10,000 in your first week. This one is different: realistic paths to your first $100, using tools that are free or cheap to start with.

The honest baseline

  • You will not make $100 in your first day. Anyone promising that is selling you something.
  • You will probably make $0 for the first 1-2 weeks. This is normal. The work is the work.
  • The first $100 is the hardest. After that, you have a system, a customer base, and a story.
  • You don't need to be technical. AI tools have made this 10x more accessible.
  • You don't need to be a US resident. All paths work globally. Payout covered at the end.

Realistic timeline: 1-4 weeks if you focus. 2-3 months if you dabble. Pick one path.

Path 1: Sell a service on Fiverr with AI

Best for: Anyone with a marketable skill — writing, design, video editing, voice-over, translation, virtual assistant, programming, marketing. AI doesn't replace the skill, it makes you 3-5x faster.

What to sell: Start with what you already know. "AI-assisted blog writing" beats "blog writing" because it's faster and more accurate. Be honest that you're using AI as a tool.

Realistic earnings: First gig: $5-25. By gig 5-10, you have repeat clients. $100 = 4-10 gigs. Most people hit $100 within 1-2 weeks of active selling.

The setup:

  1. Sign up for Fiverr (free).
  2. List 3 gigs in the first week. Different angles on the same skill.
  3. Use Beehiiv's free newsletter tools to research what buyers want.
  4. Use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini to speed up delivery. Be honest with buyers.
  5. Reply to buyer messages within 1 hour. Speed wins on Fiverr.

The catch: Fiverr takes 20% of every sale. It's a marketplace, not a long-term business — but the fastest way to your first $100.

Path 2: Start a newsletter with Beehiiv

Best for: People who like writing, have a niche interest, and can publish consistently.

How you make money: (1) Beehiiv's ad network — apply once you hit 1,000 subscribers. (2) Affiliate links to tools you recommend.

Realistic timeline: First revenue: 1-3 months. $100 in a single month: 3-6 months with weekly publishing.

The setup:

  1. Sign up for Beehiiv (free up to 2,500 subscribers).
  2. Pick a niche you know. "AI tools for [your profession]" or "[your hobby] weekly." Don't do "everything."
  3. Publish every week for 8 weeks. Consistency matters more than perfection.
  4. Use AI to draft faster, edit in your own voice. Readers can tell.
  5. Promote every issue on Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Reddit, Slack, Discord. 50% of growth is distribution.

The catch: Newsletters are a slow burn. By month 6, you have a small audience, real revenue, and a sellable asset.

Path 3: Build & sell automations with Make.com

Best for: People who like solving problems, don't mind a learning curve, and want to charge premium rates. AI automations are a hot niche — businesses pay $500-2,000 for one automation that saves them 10 hours/week.

How you make money: (1) Service — build automations for clients. (2) Templates — sell pre-built blueprints on Gumroad.

Realistic timeline: First paid gig: 2-4 weeks. $100: 1-2 gigs. $1,000/mo: 3-6 months of consistent client work.

The setup:

  1. Sign up for Make.com (free tier).
  2. Spend 1-2 weekends building 3 example automations. Document them.
  3. Post services on Fiverr (path 1!), Upwork, and a landing page on Systeme.io (free, affiliate link).
  4. Reach out to 5 small businesses per week with a specific automation idea.

The catch: Steeper learning curve. Free tier is too small for production — you'll need $9-29/mo once you serve real clients.

Path 4 (bonus): Sell digital products or templates

Best for: Anyone without a clear skill yet. AI tools (Notion, Make.com, ChatGPT) make creating a digital product a weekend project.

What to sell: Notion templates ($19-49), Make.com automation blueprints ($29-99), ChatGPT prompt packs ($9-29), or a small eBook ($5-19). Sell on Gumroad, your own site, or even a Beehiiv landing page.

Realistic timeline: First sale: 1-4 weeks. $100 in revenue: usually 5-20 sales. The advantage: zero ongoing work per sale.

The setup:

  1. Pick a template type. Notion templates are the easiest entry.
  2. Spend 1-2 weekends building 2-3 templates. Use AI for copywriting.
  3. Sell on Gumroad (free) or Systeme.io (free, affiliate link).
  4. Promote on Twitter/X, Reddit, and niche communities.

Getting paid outside the US

This is the question we get most: "Can I actually get paid for any of this from my country?" Yes — but you need the right payout rail first.

  1. Set up Payoneer first. The universal payout rail for 200+ countries. US/EU virtual bank account, accepted by Fiverr, Upwork, and most affiliate networks. Open a free account (we're not yet affiliated, but Payoneer is essential).
  2. Add Wise for currency conversion. Payoneer's conversion has a 2% markup. Send to Wise first. Mid-market rate, 0.4-1.5% fees.
  3. Connect to Fiverr, Upwork, affiliate networks. Most platforms pay out to Payoneer directly. This is the default for non-US users.
  4. For PayPal-only programs (Beehiiv, some others): Check if PayPal works in your country. Some countries have PayPal restrictions.

The full setup takes an hour and is free. Do it on day one — don't make your first $100 and not be able to withdraw it.

Common mistakes (don't do these)

  • Don't buy a course first. The free information here is more than enough. Courses are for accountability, not information.
  • Don't try all three paths at once. Pick one. Spend 4 weeks on it. Switch if it's not working.
  • Don't overthink your niche. "AI tools for accountants" is fine. Start broad, narrow as you learn.
  • Don't quit in week 1. Week 1 is the hardest. By week 3, you'll have a feel for it.
  • Don't copy "success stories" verbatim. The $5,000-in-30-days screenshots are survivorship bias.

The next step

You have four paths. Pick one. The fastest one to your first $100 is Path 1 (Fiverr service). The most compounding is Path 2 (newsletter). The highest-paying long-term is Path 3 (Make.com). Path 4 is the lowest-risk entry.

Whichever you pick: do one specific action in the next 24 hours. Sign up for the tool. List the gig. Publish the first issue. Build the first automation. The first $100 starts with one action, not another hour of research.
