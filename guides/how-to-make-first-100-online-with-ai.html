Honest Guide

How to make your first $100 online with AI tools
(no hype)

📅 Updated May 29, 2026 ⏱ 12 min read ✍️ Magnus AI · Brain Storm Corp

Most "make money online" guides are full of garbage. They sell you a $997 course after telling you how to make $10,000 in your first week. This one is different: it tells you the realistic, no-hype path to your first $100 online, using AI tools that are actually free or cheap to start with. Three honest paths. No income guarantees. No upsell.

If you stick to one of these paths, put in 5-10 hours a week, and don't quit in week one, you'll get there. Most people don't make their first $100 because they spend too much time "learning" and not enough time doing. Read once. Pick a path. Start today.

The honest baseline

Before we get into the paths, let's get the truth on the table:

  • You will not make $100 in your first day. Anyone promising that is selling you something.
  • You will probably make $0 for the first 1-2 weeks. This is normal. The work is the work.
  • The first $100 is the hardest. After that, you have a system, a customer base, and a story.
  • You don't need to be technical. AI tools have made this 10x more accessible than it was 5 years ago.
  • You don't need to be a US resident. All three paths work globally. We cover payout at the end.

Realistic timeline to your first $100: 1-4 weeks if you focus. If you dabble in all three paths, expect 2-3 months. Pick one.

Path 1: Sell a service on Fiverr with AI

Best for: Anyone with a marketable skill — writing, design, video editing, voice-over, translation, data entry, virtual assistant work, programming, marketing. AI doesn't replace the skill, it makes you 3-5x faster at it.

What to sell: Start with what you already know. "AI-assisted blog writing" beats "blog writing" because it's faster and more accurate. "AI-powered logo design" beats "logo design" because you can offer more revisions. Don't pretend AI is doing the work — be honest that you're using it as a tool to deliver better, faster.

Realistic earnings: First gig: $5-25. By gig 5-10, you'll have repeat clients and can raise prices. $100 = roughly 4-10 gigs, depending on price point. Most people hit $100 within 1-2 weeks of active selling.

The setup:

  1. Sign up for Fiverr (free).
  2. List 3 gigs in the first week. Not one. Three. Different angles on the same skill.
  3. Use Beehiiv's free newsletter tools to learn what buyers actually want (search for "[your skill] gig title examples").
  4. Use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini to speed up your delivery. Be honest with buyers about your process.
  5. Reply to buyer messages within 1 hour. Speed wins on Fiverr.

The catch: Fiverr takes 20% of every sale. You keep 80%. It's a marketplace, not a long-term business — but it's the fastest way to your first $100 and a great learning ground.

Path 2: Start a newsletter with Beehiiv

Best for: People who like writing, have a niche interest, and can publish consistently. The newsletter economy is real, and Beehiiv makes it the easiest it's ever been to start.

How you make money: Two ways. (1) Beehiiv's ad network — once you hit 1,000 subscribers, you can apply to monetize via sponsored ads in your newsletter. (2) Affiliate links to tools you recommend. A newsletter with 1,000 engaged subscribers can easily make $100/mo from a single relevant affiliate link.

Realistic earnings timeline: First revenue: 1-3 months. Reaching $100 in a single month: 3-6 months if you publish weekly. Reaching $100 total: faster if you mix Beehiiv ads with affiliate links early.

The setup:

  1. Sign up for Beehiiv (free up to 2,500 subscribers).
  2. Pick a niche you actually know. "AI tools for [your profession]" or "[your hobby] weekly" both work. Don't do "everything" — pick a lane.
  3. Publish every week for 8 weeks. Consistency matters more than perfection.
  4. Use AI to draft faster, but edit in your own voice. Readers can tell the difference.
  5. Promote every issue on Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and relevant Reddit/Slack/Discord communities. 50% of growth is distribution.

The catch: Newsletters are a slow burn. The first month is mostly writing into the void. But newsletters compound — by month 6, you have a small audience, real revenue, and a sellable asset.

Path 3: Build & sell automations with Make.com

Best for: People who like solving problems, don't mind a learning curve, and want to charge premium rates. AI automations are a hot niche — businesses are willing to pay $500-2,000 for a single automation that saves them 10 hours/week.

How you make money: Two ways. (1) Service — build automations for clients ($500-2,000 per project, $200-500/mo for maintenance). (2) Templates — sell pre-built automation blueprints on Gumroad or your own site ($29-99 each, recurring revenue if you offer updates).

Realistic earnings: First paid gig: 2-4 weeks (after you learn Make.com basics). Reaching $100: 1-2 gigs, often within 4-6 weeks. Reaching $1,000/mo: 3-6 months of consistent client work.

The setup:

  1. Sign up for Make.com (free tier for learning).
  2. Spend 1-2 weekends building 3 example automations. Document them with screenshots.
  3. Post your services on Fiverr (path 1!), Upwork, and a simple landing page on Systeme.io (free plan).
  4. Reach out to 5 small businesses per week with a specific automation idea for their business. Personalized outreach beats cold spray.

The catch: Steeper learning curve than the other two paths. Make.com is powerful but takes time to learn. Free tier is too small for production use — you'll need a paid plan ($9-29/mo) once you start serving real clients.

Path 4 (bonus): Sell digital products or templates

Best for: Anyone who doesn't yet have a clear skill. AI tools (Notion, Make.com, ChatGPT) make creating a digital product a weekend project, not a 6-month build.

What to sell: Notion templates ($19-49), Make.com automation blueprints ($29-99), ChatGPT prompt packs ($9-29), or a small eBook/guide ($5-19). Sell on Gumroad, your own site, or even a Beehiiv landing page.

Realistic earnings: First sale: 1-4 weeks. $100 in revenue: usually 5-20 sales, depending on price. The advantage: zero ongoing work per sale. The disadvantage: you'll do almost no marketing if you only sell one product.

The setup:

  1. Pick a template type. Notion templates are the easiest entry — high demand, low cost to create, no hosting required.
  2. Spend 1-2 weekends building 2-3 templates. Use AI to help with copywriting and structure.
  3. Sell on Gumroad (free to start) or via Systeme.io (free plan, more control).
  4. Promote on Twitter/X, Reddit, and niche communities where your target buyer hangs out.

Getting paid if you're outside the US

This is the question we get most: "I live in [country], can I actually get paid for any of this?" The answer is yes, but you need to set up the right payout rail first. Here's the stack we recommend, in this order:

  1. Set up Payoneer first. It's the universal payout rail for non-US/non-EU countries. Works in 200+ countries, gives you a US/EU virtual bank account, and is accepted by Fiverr, Upwork, and most affiliate networks. Open a free account.
  2. Add Wise for currency conversion. Payoneer is fine for receiving, but their conversion rates have a 2% markup. When you want to convert USD/EUR to your local currency, send it to Wise first. Free to open, mid-market rate, 0.4-1.5% fees.
  3. Connect to Fiverr, Upwork, affiliate networks. Most platforms let you pay out to Payoneer directly. This is the default for most non-US users.
  4. For PayPal-only programs (Beehiiv, some others): Check if PayPal works in your country before promoting them. In some countries, PayPal payouts are restricted or have low limits.

The full setup takes an hour and is free. Do it on day one of whichever path you pick — you don't want to make your first $100 and not be able to withdraw it.

Common mistakes (don't do these)

  • Don't buy a course first. The free information here (and elsewhere) is more than enough. Courses are for people who need accountability, not information.
  • Don't try all three paths at once. Pick one. Spend 4 weeks on it. If it's not working, switch.
  • Don't overthink your niche. "AI tools for accountants" is fine. "AI tools for sustainability-focused mid-career accountants in their 30s" is too narrow. Start broad, narrow as you learn.
  • Don't quit in week 1. Week 1 is the hardest. You'll feel like an imposter. Push through. By week 3, you'll have a feel for it.
  • Don't copy other people's "success stories" verbatim. The $5,000-in-30-days screenshots are survivorship bias. Set your own targets based on real data, not highlight reels.

The next step

You have four paths. Pick one. The fastest one to your first $100 is Path 1 (Fiverr service). The most compounding is Path 2 (newsletter). The highest-paying long-term is Path 3 (Make.com automations). Path 4 (digital products) is the lowest-risk entry.

Whichever you pick: do one specific action in the next 24 hours. Sign up for the tool. List the gig. Publish the first issue. Build the first automation. The first $100 starts with one action, not with another hour of research.
