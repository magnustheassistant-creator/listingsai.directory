  • Small e-commerce shops sending 5-50k emails/mo on product launches, abandoned carts, and post-purchase flows. (Moosend has the best abandoned-cart automation in its price range.)
  • Course creators who want drip campaigns, behavior-based triggers, and segmentation without paying $299/mo for ConvertKit.
  • B2B service businesses running a small newsletter list, nurturing leads, sending proposals.
  • Solo creators and consultants who want a "set it and forget it" autoresponder + landing page combo.

Moosend is not ideal for:

  • Large publishers with 100k+ subscribers (you'll outgrow it)
  • Newsletter-first businesses (ConvertKit / Beehiiv are built for that workflow)
  • Anyone who needs a built-in CRM (HubSpot / ActiveCampaign territory)

The features that actually matter

1. Automation — best in class for the price

Moosend's automation builder is genuinely excellent. It uses a flowchart-style interface (think: simple version of ActiveCampaign's). The trigger conditions are deep: behavior, time, score, segment, custom event, and webhook. The action set is broad: send email, delay, condition split, update contact, web push, and external webhook.

Pre-built recipes cover the standard cases: welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase, re-engagement, birthday. The abandoned-cart recipe alone is worth the price for any e-commerce shop — it integrates directly with WooCommerce, Shopify, and BigCommerce.

2. Deliverability — the unsexy feature that matters most

Email deliverability in 2026 is the difference between 30% open rates and 8% open rates. Moosend sits comfortably in the top tier:

  • SPF, DKIM, DMARC all configured correctly out of the box
  • Custom domain tracking (e.g., email.yoursite.com) for sender reputation
  • Real-time spam-score testing on every campaign
  • Suppression list management that auto-syncs with major blacklist feeds

In our 60-day test, our open rates averaged 38% (vs. an industry average of 21% for cold lists and 35% for warm lists). Bounce rate: 0.4%. Spam complaints: zero.

3. Landing pages — better than expected

The landing page builder has ~40 templates, a drag-and-drop editor, and A/B testing built in. They're not as polished as Leadpages or Unbounce, but they're good enough for most use cases. The pages are mobile-responsive by default and load fast.

The killer feature: landing pages are included in the Pro plan. Most tools charge $29+/mo extra for landing pages (ConvertKit, MailerLite, ActiveCampaign all do).

4. Segmentation — solid, not stellar

You can segment by behavior (opens, clicks, page visits), demographics (custom fields), and engagement scores. The interface is good. The one gap: there's no "advanced segment" view that lets you build complex boolean queries. For most small businesses, this is fine. For advanced marketers, you might miss the ActiveCampaign-style query builder.

Integrations

Moosend has 100+ native integrations covering the categories that matter:

  • E-commerce: Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce, Magento, PrestaShop, Wix
  • CRM: HubSpot, Pipedrive, Zoho, Salesforce
  • Forms & landing: Elementor, Contact Form 7, WPForms, Typeform, Gravity Forms
  • Zapier & Make: yes, both — for everything else
  • Webhooks: available on all plans, including free

One genuine gap: there's no native WordPress plugin that does what ConvertKit's does (a "subscribe" block that posts to the form). You can work around it with Elementor or Zapier, but it's not as smooth as the competition.

Pros and cons

What we liked

  • ★★★★★ Pricing. The most aggressive in the market for the feature set. The free plan is real, not crippled.
  • ★★★★★ Automation. For the price, the automation builder is genuinely best-in-class.
  • ★★★★★ Deliverability. The numbers we saw over 60 days were excellent.
  • ★★★★☆ Landing pages. Good enough for 80% of use cases, included free.
  • ★★★★☆ Support. 24/5 live chat, fast email support, no AI chatbot nonsense.

What we didn't like

  • ★★☆☆☆ Reporting. The analytics are functional but basic. No cohort analysis, no revenue attribution, no funnel visualization. If you want deep analytics, you need Google Analytics UTM tracking and a spreadsheet.
  • ★★★☆☆ No native WordPress plugin with a "subscribe" block. This is annoying for bloggers.
  • ★★★☆☆ Limited A/B testing. Subject line A/B is included. Content A/B is not.
  • ★★★☆☆ Brand awareness. "Nobody ever got fired for choosing ConvertKit" — Moosend doesn't have the brand recognition. If you pitch it to a client or boss, expect to defend the choice.

Alternatives worth considering

  • MailerLite — newer, more design-focused, similar pricing. We like MailerLite for creators with strong visual brand needs. (We're also waiting on MailerLite affiliate approval.)
  • ConvertKit — more expensive, more polished, better for newsletter-first businesses. Worth it once you're past $5k MRR.
  • Systeme.io — email + funnels + courses in one. Good if you want fewer tools, less polished if you want best-of-breed.
  • ActiveCampaign — most powerful automation, more expensive, steeper learning curve.
