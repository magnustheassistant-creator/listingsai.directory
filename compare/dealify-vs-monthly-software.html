Dealify (Lifetime Deals) vs Monthly Software: The Real Math

"Pay once, own forever" sounds like a scam because most of the time it is. But sometimes it's a 90% discount. Here's how to tell the difference.

Quick verdict: Lifetime deals on Dealify are a real 70-95% discount IF the tool survives 24+ months and you actually use it. They are a 100% waste of money if the tool shuts down in year 1 or you only use it twice. The decision rule: only buy a LTD for tools you'd pay the monthly price for anyway AND that have been operating 2+ years.

What is Dealify?

Dealify is a curated marketplace for "lifetime deals" (LTDs) on SaaS software. The model: SaaS companies who need early traction offer a one-time payment deal (e.g., $69 for what would normally be $29/mo) in exchange for upfront cash. Dealify curates, vets, and refund-warrants the deals. You pay once, you have the tool forever (theoretically).

The marketplace has 200+ active deals and 60-day refunds on new deals. Think of it as a "Group Buy" model applied to software, with the platform taking a cut.

The math: when lifetime deals win

Let's say a tool normally costs $29/mo ($348/yr). On Dealify, the same tool might be available for a one-time $69.

  • Year 1 savings: $279 (you pay $69 vs. $348)
  • Year 2 savings: $348 (you pay $0 vs. $348)
  • Year 3+ savings: $348/yr (compounding)
  • 5-year savings: $1,431 on a single $69 deal

The breakeven point is month 3. After that, every month is pure savings. This works for tools you already use, will use for years, and that don't suddenly double in price (LTDs typically grandfather your plan; new features may require upgrading to a monthly plan).

The risks: when lifetime deals lose

There are 4 real risks:

  1. Vendor shutdown. SaaS companies fail at 30-50% rates in years 1-3. If the tool shuts down, you lose the deal entirely. Dealify's 60-day refund mitigates this, but doesn't help if the tool dies in month 8.
  2. Feature freeze. Vendors often stop developing the LTD tier once they have enough monthly subscribers. You get the v1 of the product forever; the v2, v3, v4 features require upgrading to monthly.
  3. Support decay. LTD users are typically deprioritized in support queues. You might wait days for a response that monthly subscribers get in hours.
  4. You don't actually use it. Most common failure. You buy 15 LTDs in a Dealify sale, use 2 of them, and the other 13 are 100% waste. "Lifetime" is meaningless if you never open the tool.

The decision rule

Buy a lifetime deal ONLY if all 4 are true:

  1. You've already been paying monthly for the tool (or a competitor) — or you have a real, specific use case planned for month 1
  2. The tool has been operating 2+ years with steady funding/revenue
  3. The LTD includes the features you actually need (read the fine print — some LTDs are stripped-down versions)
  4. You'd be comfortable losing the money if the vendor shuts down in 18 months

If any of those are no, pay monthly. The discount isn't worth the risk.

Categories where LTDs usually win

  • SEO tools (keyword research, backlink trackers) — most mature SaaS in this category is stable, and a single $69-149 LTD replaces a $99-299/mo subscription
  • Image/video tools (compressors, converters, AI generators) — once you're using them, you use them forever
  • Form builders, popup tools — commodity SaaS, low vendor risk, high LTD value
  • Email templates, design assets — non-vendor-dependent; once downloaded, yours
  • Productivity tools, note-taking, project management — sticky once you migrate your workflow in

Categories where LTDs usually lose

  • Funnel builders, page builders — competitive space, vendor risk is high, you'll likely outgrow the LTD tier
  • Email marketing — the deliverability & list size are what you're paying for; LTDs are often capped at 500-1,000 contacts and you'll exceed that in year 1
  • Course platforms, membership sites — vendor lock-in is severe; if they shut down, your students lose access
  • AI tools — moving too fast; the AI tool you buy today may be obsolete in 6 months (LTD doesn't help with that)
  • Anything that depends on integrations with other tools — integration maintenance is a common cost-cut when vendors run out of money

The alternative: monthly subscriptions

Paying monthly is the conservative, "no surprises" approach. You pay for what you use, you can cancel anytime, and you always have the latest version.

When monthly is better:

  • You don't know if you'll use the tool 6 months from now
  • You're trying a new tool category for the first time
  • The tool is in a fast-moving space (AI, crypto) where v2 might be wildly different
  • You have cash flow constraints (don't tie up $500 in 5 LTDs that might not pan out)

When monthly is worse:

  • You've been paying monthly for 2+ years and use the tool daily (you've already paid for the LTD multiple times over)
  • You're a small business where every $29/mo is meaningful (a stack of 10 tools at $29/mo = $3,480/yr — same as a big annual software purchase)

About Dealify's 60-day refund

Dealify offers a 60-day refund window on new deals. This is much better than most LTD marketplaces (Appsumo historically had 60 days but the vendor has to approve, which is hit-or-miss). It means: if you buy a deal and the tool turns out to be a disaster, you can get your money back within 60 days, no questions asked.

It does not protect against vendor shutdown at month 8. For that, you need to vet the vendor yourself (check their funding, revenue, age, and reviews on G2/Capterra).

Final verdict

Buy from Dealify if:

  • You're replacing a monthly subscription you already pay for
  • The tool is in a stable, mature category (SEO, design, productivity)
  • You have specific projects in mind for the tool (not aspirational "someday")
  • You're willing to lose the money if the vendor fails

Pay monthly if:

  • You're trying a new tool for the first time
  • The tool is in a fast-moving space (AI, crypto, social)
  • You need the latest features / integrations
  • You can't afford to lose the money
