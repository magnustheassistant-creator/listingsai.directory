How to evaluate online tools
(without the marketing BS)

Head-to-head comparisons are mostly affiliate-driven noise. Here's how to actually evaluate a tool yourself — what to look for, what to ignore, and the questions to ask before paying.

1. Is the free tier actually usable?

Most "free" tools aren't free — they have aggressive limits that force you to upgrade within a week. The honest test: can you complete a real workflow (build a funnel, send an email, publish a post) without hitting a paywall?

Real free plans (no card): Systeme.io (2,000 contacts), Beehiiv (2,500 subscribers), Make.com (1,000 ops/mo), Notion (personal), Fiverr (to sell).

2. Is the price transparent?

If the pricing page requires a "Contact Sales" or "Get a Demo" button, the price is probably high and negotiable. For self-serve tools, the price should be on the public page, all-in, with no hidden transaction fees or per-recipient charges.

Watch out: "Free" tools that bill per email sent, per contact stored, or per transaction processed. The bill can balloon fast.

3. Does it work outside the US?

Many tools are US-only or have region-locked features. If you're in SEA, Latin America, or Africa, the "Works globally?" check matters. Look at: payment methods accepted, language support, data residency, and customer support hours.

Quick test: Search for "[tool name] + [your country]" reviews. If they're all from 3+ years ago, the tool probably doesn't actively support your region.

4. Is the company stable?

Look for: how long they've been around, recent funding or revenue signals, team size, public roadmap. A tool that pivots every 6 months is risky. A tool with no updates for 2 years is risky too.

Quick check: Crunchbase for funding, LinkedIn for team size, Twitter for product updates.

5. Is the affiliate program honest?

When we list a tool as affiliate-supported, we check: commission rate, cookie length, payout threshold, payout methods, and refund clawback policy. A program that pays 50% but claws back on refunds isn't 50%.

For buyers: The price you pay is the same whether you use our affiliate link or not. Affiliate commissions come out of the company's marketing budget, not yours.
