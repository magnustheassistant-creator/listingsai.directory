Make.com — the new automation champion

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is the more powerful tool. The visual flow-chart builder lets you do things Zapier can't — error handling, branching, data transformation, AI agent orchestration. Pricing is fairer (you see exactly what each operation costs). The 35% commission is also much higher than Zapier's tiered 15-30%.

Where Make.com wins:

  • Visual flow-chart builder. You can see the entire workflow, not just a linear list. Handles complex logic, error branches, and data manipulation Zapier can't.
  • Per-operation pricing is honest. You see what each step costs. Zapier's "task" model hides complexity.
  • 35% recurring for life vs Zapier's 15-30% for 12 months. The math is dramatically better for affiliates.
  • Better payout for non-US users. Make pays via Wise — the cleanest international payout rail. Zapier payouts vary by country.
  • Built for AI workflows. If you want to build AI agents that take actions across multiple apps, Make is the only realistic option at this price point.

Where Make.com falls short:

  • Steeper learning curve. The visual builder is more powerful but harder to learn than Zapier's linear approach.
  • Smaller app ecosystem. 1,500+ integrations vs 6,000+ for Zapier. Some niche apps are missing.
  • Free tier is too small for production. 1,000 ops/mo sounds like a lot until you realize a single complex automation can use 50+ ops per run.

Zapier — the established leader

Zapier has been the default "connect two apps" tool since 2011. It works. It has the largest app directory. If you need a quick, simple automation between two well-known apps, Zapier is the easiest path.

Where Zapier wins:

  • 6,000+ app integrations. The largest by a wide margin. If a SaaS tool has an API, it's probably on Zapier first.
  • Simpler builder. Linear Zaps are easier to teach and learn. Good for non-technical users.
  • Brand recognition. Most non-technical users have heard of Zapier. Easier to recommend to a non-technical audience.

Where Zapier falls short:

  • Per-task pricing is sneaky. A "task" is a Zap step, and complex Zaps have many steps. Costs add up.
  • Lower commission, shorter duration. 15-30% for 12 months vs Make's 35% for life. You earn less, for less time.
  • Less powerful. Can't handle complex branching, error handling, or data transformation that Make can.
  • No visual workflow view. Linear Zaps get confusing when you have 10+ steps.

The verdict

For automation power users and agencies: Make.com. More powerful, fairer pricing, better payout for international users, and much better commission economics for affiliates. For non-technical users who need a simple "send this to that" automation: Zapier is still easier to learn. The right tool depends on the complexity of the workflow and the technical level of the user.

If you're choosing which to recommend to your audience, the answer depends on their skill level. For tech-savvy readers and businesses with complex workflows, Make.com. For non-technical readers who just need "if X happens, send Y," Zapier still wins on ease of use.
