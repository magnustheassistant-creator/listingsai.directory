Our mission

The internet is full of fake review sites, AI-generated "best of" listicles, and affiliate pages that exist only to earn commissions. We're building the opposite: a focused directory of tools we'd recommend to a friend, with honest pros and cons, real "works globally" notes, and a clear stance on what we don't cover.

We focus on one audience — people who want to earn or save money online — and we say no to categories that don't fit. That means we don't list credit-repair programs, financial-services lead networks, or trading platforms. Those need real credentials and proper compliance.

How we choose what to list

Every tool on ListingsAI is reviewed against our editorial checklist before it gets recommended:

  • Does it actually work? We sign up. We test core features. If the "free" plan is unusable, we say so.
  • Is the affiliate program honest? Realistic commission rates, transparent terms, reliable payouts.
  • Does it work outside the US? We flag US-only or region-limited programs because most readers aren't in the US.
  • Is the company stable? No fly-by-night vendors, no obvious scams, no disappearing acts.
  • Would we use it? The final test. If we wouldn't recommend it to a friend, we don't list it.

E-E-A-T compliance

Google's quality guidelines flag money-and-income content for extra scrutiny (YMYL — Your Money or Your Life). We follow E-E-A-T principles:

Experience

We actually test the tools. We verify affiliate links, sign up for free plans, and check the end-to-end experience.

Expertise

We research industry standards, commission benchmarks, payout policies, and how each program compares to alternatives.

Authoritativeness

Named company (Brain Storm Corp), real contact email, transparent about-page, consistent editorial voice.

Trustworthiness

Full affiliate disclosure. No fake testimonials. No paid placements.

Transparency

Clear about which tools we earn from and which are informational only. Updated quarterly.

No income guarantees

Realistic framing. We tell you "some users earn X" not "you'll make $5,000 guaranteed."

What we don't list

Some categories pay well but are bad for a new, independent site to be in. We skip them entirely:

  • Credit repair & financial services — YMYL trust and compliance risk.
  • Trading platforms (eToro, Plus500, etc.) — financial promotion is restricted in several SEA countries.
  • Cashback sites (TopCashback, Rakuten) — region-locked.
  • Get-rich-quick schemes — anything that promises "guaranteed income."
  • Multi-level marketing (MLM) — we don't promote programs that pay for recruitment.
