About

We test AI tools so you don't waste money on the wrong ones

ListingsAI is a small, independent review site built by one person who's tired of SEO-bait "best AI tools" lists that exist only to harvest affiliate clicks.

The problem we're fixing

If you've ever Googled "best AI tools for X" or "how to make money online," you know the problem. The first ten results are nearly identical: a list of 15-25 tools, with the same vague praise, the same broken pros/cons formatting, the same affiliate links that earn the site owner 30-50% when you sign up. Most of them are written by people who've never used the tools. Some are written by AI. The reviews read like press releases.

The reason: affiliate commissions pay more than honest writing. Most of these sites optimize for affiliate revenue, not for helping you. They list whatever pays the highest commission, regardless of whether the tool is right for you.

ListingsAI is built the opposite way. We start with the question "what would actually help someone earn or save money?" and only then do we check whether the answer happens to have an affiliate program. If it doesn't, we still list it. If it pays us to list a tool that doesn't help you, we don't list it.

Our editorial standards

Every tool on ListingsAI goes through the same process:

  1. We use it. Not a 14-day trial, not a demo account — real usage for at least 30 days, usually 90. We pay for it ourselves when we can. When we can't, we use free tiers or trial accounts and disclose that.
  2. We write what we found. Pros, cons, pricing surprises, support quality, refund policy. We name competitors we'd actually pick over the tool if it didn't exist.
  3. We disclose everything. If we earn a commission, the listing has an AFFILIATE badge. If a tool gave us a free account, we say so. If we got a press kit, we say so. If we have a personal relationship with the founder, we say so.
  4. We update reviews. Tools get better and worse. A tool that was great in 2024 might be abandoned in 2026. We re-test our top recommendations every 6 months and update the reviews.
  5. We say no to most tools. The "AI tools" space has thousands of products. Maybe 50 are worth your time. Maybe 20 are worth your money. Most of the directory is "we evaluated this, didn't list it" — but we don't write those up because the SEO value is negative.

What "honest" means to us

Honest doesn't mean we have to hate everything. It means:

  • We say so when a tool is good, and we say so when it's bad.
  • We don't bury the bad in a paragraph below the good.
  • We acknowledge when our affiliate status influences our opinion, and we work actively against that.
  • We name tools we don't recommend and explain why. (See our essay on why most affiliate sites fail for the full philosophy.)

Why we built the site as a directory, not a blog

Most affiliate sites are blogs that pretend to be directories. A blog post titled "The 17 Best AI Tools for X" is just a long sales page. A directory is structured: every tool has a card, every card has the same fields, and you can compare tools directly without scrolling through 4,000 words of filler.

We chose the directory structure because it's actually useful. The articles on this site are guides, how-tos, and reviews — long-form, but anchored in something specific, not SEO bait.
