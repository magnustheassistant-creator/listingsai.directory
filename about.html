Our mission

The internet is full of fake review sites, AI-generated "best of" listicles, and affiliate pages that exist only to earn commissions. We're building the opposite: a small, focused directory of tools we'd actually recommend to a friend, with honest pros and cons, real "works globally" notes, and a clear stance on what we don't cover.

We focus on one audience — people who want to earn or save money online — and we say no to categories that don't fit. That means we don't list credit-repair programs, financial-services lead networks, or trading platforms. Those need real credentials and proper compliance, and a new site like ours shouldn't be in that business.

How we choose what to list

Every tool on ListingsAI is reviewed against our editorial checklist before it gets recommended:

  • Does it actually work? We test signup, free tier, and core features. If the "free" plan is unusable, we say so.
  • Is the affiliate program honest? Realistic commission rates, transparent terms, reliable payouts.
  • Does it work outside the US? Many tools are US-only or have region-locked features. We flag this because most of our readers aren't in the US.
  • Is the company stable? No fly-by-night vendors, no obvious scams, no disappearing acts.
  • Would we use it? This is the final test. If we wouldn't recommend it to a friend, we don't list it.

Read the full methodology on our How We Review page.

E-E-A-T compliance

Google's quality guidelines flag money-and-income content for extra scrutiny (YMYL — Your Money or Your Life). We follow E-E-A-T principles: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness.

Experience

We actually test the tools. We verify affiliate links work, sign up for free plans, and check what the experience is like end-to-end. If a tool has friction we don't mention, it's because we missed it — tell us.

Expertise

We research industry standards, commission benchmarks, payout policies, and how each program compares to alternatives. We tell you when a program is good, when it's bad, and when it's oversold.

Authoritativeness

We operate under a named company (Brain Storm Corp), with a real contact email, transparent about-page, and consistent editorial voice. We're not anonymous.

Trustworthiness

Full affiliate disclosure on every page. Clear contact information. No fake testimonials. No paid placements. We list only what we'd recommend.

What we don't list

Some categories pay well but are bad for a new, independent site to get into. We skip them entirely:

  • Credit repair & financial services — YMYL trust and compliance risk. Real credentials required.
  • Trading platforms (eToro, Plus500, etc.) — financial promotion is restricted in several SEA countries. Real legal exposure.
  • Cashback sites (TopCashback, Rakuten) — region-locked, not a fit for our global audience.
  • Get-rich-quick schemes — anything that promises "guaranteed income" or "make $5,000 in 7 days."
