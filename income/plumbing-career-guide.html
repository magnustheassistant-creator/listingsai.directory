How to Become a Plumber

Step 1: Meet Basic Requirements

Most plumber apprenticeship programs require: minimum age 18, high school diploma or GED, valid driver's license, physical ability to lift 50+ lbs.

Step 2: Choose Your Path

  • Apprenticeship (4-5 years): Earn while you learn. Join a union (UA - United Association) or non-union program. Typically starts at $15-$20/hour.
  • Trade School + Apprenticeship (2+4 years): Start with a 1-2 year plumbing certificate program, then enter a reduced apprenticeship.

Step 3: Get Licensed

After completing apprenticeship, you must pass your state's journeyman plumber exam. Requirements generally include: completion of apprenticeship program, passing the state licensing exam, proof of insurance and bond.

Plumbing License Levels:

  • Apprentice — In training, works under journeyman
  • Journeyman — Licensed to work independently
  • Master Plumber — Top license, can own business, design systems
