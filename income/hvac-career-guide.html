Step 1: Understand the Training Paths

There are two main paths to becoming an HVAC technician. Each has different costs, timelines, and pros/cons.

Path A: Trade School

Enroll in an accredited HVAC training program at a community college or private trade school. Programs typically last 6-18 months and cost $5,000-$20,000.

  • Pros: Structured learning, hands-on labs, job placement assistance
  • Cons: Tuition costs, full-time commitment needed
  • Best for: Career changers, people wanting fast entry

Path B: Apprenticeship

Join an apprenticeship program (often through unions or contractor associations). Earn while you learn over 3-5 years. Apprenticeships typically pay $15-$25/hour starting.

  • Pros: Paid training, no tuition, direct industry experience
  • Cons: Longer timeline, lower starting pay, competitive entry
  • Best for: Those who can't afford tuition, prefer earning while learning

Step 2: Get Certified

All HVAC technicians need EPA Section 608 Certification to handle refrigerants. This is a federal requirement.

Required Certifications:

  • EPA 608 Certification — Required for all refrigerant work. Type I (small appliances), Type II (high pressure), Type III (low pressure), Universal (all types).
  • State Licenses — Most states require journeyman or master HVAC license. Requirements vary by state.
  • NATE Certification — Optional but highly respected. North American Technician Excellence certification shows advanced knowledge.

Step 4: Start Your Career

After certification, you'll start as an entry-level technician working under an experienced HVAC tech.

Career Paths After Certification:

  • Residential HVAC Tech — Work in homes, average $45K-$65K/year
  • Commercial HVAC Tech — Work in buildings, average $55K-$80K/year
  • HVAC Installer — New construction, average $50K-$70K/year
  • Service Manager — Manage teams, average $70K-$100K/year
  • Start Your Own Business — Highest earning potential

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to become an HVAC technician?

Most HVAC training programs take 6-18 months to complete. Accelerated programs can get you certified in as little as 6 months, while apprenticeship programs may take 3-5 years.

How much does HVAC training cost?

HVAC training costs range from $5,000 to $20,000 depending on the program. Community college programs typically cost $5,000-$10,000. Financial aid, grants, and payment plans are often available.

What is the average HVAC technician salary?

Entry-level HVAC technicians earn $40,000-$55,000 per year. With 3-5 years of experience, salaries typically reach $55,000-$75,000. Master HVAC technicians can earn $75,000-$100,000+ annually.

Do you need a license to work as an HVAC technician?

Licensing requirements vary by state. Most states require EPA Section 608 certification for handling refrigerants. Many states also require a journeyman or master HVAC license.
